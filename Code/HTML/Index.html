Status of Desertification in India

97.85 Million Hectares

Total degraded land area in the country, affecting millions of livelihoods.

Vulnerable States

Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra, and parts of Punjab face severe threats.

Root Causes

Deforestation

Clearing forests for urbanization removes root systems that hold topsoil together.

Overgrazing

Intensive grazing strips land of vegetation cover, accelerating soil erosion.

Unsustainable Farming

Heavy reliance on chemicals and improper irrigation leads to soil salinity.

Pathways to Restoration

Aravalli Green Wall

A proposed 1,400km long ecological corridor acting as a vital green buffer against the expanding Thar Desert.

Water Harvesting

Reviving traditional stepwells (Baoris) and deploying modern micro-irrigation to replenish shrinking aquifers.

Agroforestry

Integrating resilient native trees into farming systems to stabilize topsoil, retain moisture, and improve yields.

Decoding the Data

Looking at the analytics, water erosion emerges as the leading culprit behind land degradation in India, quietly washing away vital topsoil layer by layer. This is closely followed by the rapid loss of natural vegetation, which strips the earth of its protective cover and accelerates wind erosion. Geographically, Rajasthan bears the heaviest burden, with over 21 million hectares affected due to its arid climate and the expanding Thar Desert. However, the high vulnerability of states like Maharashtra and Gujarat highlights that this crisis extends far beyond traditional desert regions, threatening agricultural heartlands nationwide.

The Path Forward

Currently, nearly a third of our nation's land requires immediate healing and intervention.

We must urgently expand ecological buffers like the Aravalli Green Wall to halt the creeping sands.
Transitioning to smart agroforestry will anchor the topsoil and restore vital moisture naturally.
Communities must be empowered to revive ancient rainwater harvesting techniques alongside modern micro-irrigation.
Protecting our foundation requires a unified shift from relentless exploitation to mindful, regenerative stewardship.
